My Process

NOF Microsite

This project was done during my second year at SFU for IAT235, a course for Information Design. As a group of 4, we were asked to design a microsite for the Switzerland based performing art organization Novel Opera Fribourg (NOF) with an expectation to combine the visual style of the company with Dan Friedman’s. My role in this project was to enhance the visual and design interaction for the homepage and shows page.

As seen from the low fidelity wireframe in Figure 1, I started with a layout that we agreed on as a group. Later combined the use of halftone image, repetition of shapes, and decomposing typefaces from Friedman with NOF’s visual element at the centre of our homepage to bring a strong impression and unity in style. However, soon I discovered that the expressive typeface in the centre became a visual hotspot that made it hard to incorporate the home page menu button with a clear affordance as shown in Figure 2.

Failed to find a solution at the moment, I decided to move on to the next stage. After several experimentation on color combination as Figure 3 displays, I realized that color could become a useful indicator for user interaction in this case.

In the final iteration, I ended up hiding the menu within the typography which will be trigger through mouse-hovering interaction over each line of text, so that the user navigation is playfully connected to the decomposed typography which will encourage users to explore the site. Meanwhile, I used a split complementary colour scheme to highlight each selected menu title as shown in Figure 4 in order to create visual hierarchy and variety.

Furthermore, to maintain the visual and interactive consistency throughout site, I also applied the hovering method in the shows page to encourage users’ active exploration of the show list. As shown in Figure 5, when hovering over images it would activates a specific color for the halftone image icon, which is chosen according to the opera’s theme allowing users to get a first impression of the show.

In conclusion, I am proud with my contribution to this project as I get to incorporate Friedman’s “unpredicatablity” in this microsite through playful interactive and visual elements. Most importantly, I learned that there is always a solution to design challenges and all it takes are time and exploration.
